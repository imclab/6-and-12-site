About the Instrument

Gibson's iconic EDS-1275 is commonly known as the "doubleneck SG". While the SG is a popular and successful guitar, the Les Paul remains Gibon's most adored instrument. A doubleneck version of the Les Paul has never been produced; 6 and 12 is an interpretation of that instrument.

6 and 12 is a doubleneck electric guitar with a standard six string neck and a baritone twelve string neck. Its construction is similar to that of a Les Paul; 6 and 12 has a bound, carved body and symmetric, angled headstocks.

It takes aesthetic cues from the '69 Les Paul Custom; 6 and 12 has an added middle pickup on the six string neck, three-ply cream binding, gold hardware, ebony fretboards and a glossy black finish.

6 and 12 also abandons some of the Les Paul's conventions: the body's symmetry is offset, the switches are located between the potentiometers, and the top's geometry is changed to accomodate the second neck.
